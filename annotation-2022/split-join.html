Splitting and joining words

  • Items that are split
  • Items treated as unitary
  • Items treated as written
  • Differences among the corpora Dollar tag, Genitive/possessive modifiers of N)

Certain items in later English are fusions of earlier multi-word phrases. Given the length of time covered by the diachronic corpora and because word division in early texts is not always well represented, these items are difficult to treat in a consistent way across time. We attempt to adhere to the following principles and strategies.

  • Certain forms are always split due to the requirements of the annotation.

  • Items that fused before Middle English are treated as unitary.

  • Items that are not completely fused by Middle English are generally treated as written. That is, the components receive separate POS tags when spelled apart. When spelled together, they receive a complex POS tag to reflect their phrasal origin.

  • A small number of cases are treated differently in the later corpora than in the PPCME2 because certain expressions undergo reanalysis and in order to facilitate the lemmatization of the later corpora.

Items that are split

When an orthographic word in the original text belongs to different sentence-level or phrasal constituents, the word is always split. The location of the split is marked by a trailing "@" on the first component and a leading "@" on the second component.

  • FOR + TO 
    (FOR for-@) (TO @to)
(FOR for@) (TO @te)
  • Modal + BE 
    (MD shal@) (BE @be)
(MD wol@) (BE @bee)
(MD wyl@) (BE @be)
  • Modal + negation 
    (MD can@) (NEG @not)
(MD ca@) (NEG @nt)
(MD ca@) (NEG @n't)
(MD wo@) (NEG @n't)
  • Modal or verb + pronoun 
    (MD maist@) (PRO @tow)
(VBP keth@) (PRO @a)		← spelling variant of QUOTHA (= QUOTH HE)
(VBP (Pre@) (PRO @the)
(VBP (Pri@) (PRO @thee)
(VBP quoth@) (PRO @a)
  • Negation + modal or verb (in Middle English) 
    (NEG n@) (BEP @is)		← NE + IS
(NEG n@) (BED @aes)		← NE + WAS
(NEG n@) (HVP abbeo+d)		← NE + HAVETH
(NEG n@) (MD @ulle)		← NE + WILL
(NEG n@) (MD @alde)		← NE + WOULD
(NEG n@) (VBD @uste)		← NE + WUSTE
  • Preposition + multiword complement 
    (PP (P o'@)
    (NP (PRO$ @my) (N life)))
  • Pronoun + modal or verb 
    (PRO i@) (MD @challe)		← dialect form of I SHALL 
(PRO it@) (BEP @s)
(PRO it@) (BEP @'s)
(PRO me@) (VBP thinckes)
(PRO me@) (VBP thynketh)
(EX ther@) (BEP @s)
(PRO they@) (BEP @'l)
(PRO 'T@) (BEP @is)
(PRO t'@) (BEP @is)
(PRO t@) (MDP @will)

Items treated as unitary

These items are generally spelled in the original texts as a single orthographic word. When spelled as several words, they are joined by underscores in the annotated version.

(ADJ a_lone)
