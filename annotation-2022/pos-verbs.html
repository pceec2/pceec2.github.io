Auxiliary and main verb use of BE, DO, and HAVE

Forms of the verbs BE, DO, and HAVE are distinguished from all other verbs, but the POS tags do not distinguish the auxiliary and main verb uses of these verbs. In the parsed files, auxiliary forms of BE and HAVE can be distinguished from main verb forms by the presence of a (possibly silent) articiple in the clause (a perfect or passive participle for BE, and a perfect participle for HAVE). Periphrastic DO is always accompanied by a (possibly silent) infinitive.

For ease of retrieval, contracted forms that are ambiguous between BE and HAVE (He's come) are tagged by default as BE.

Modals

The following items can count as modals:
CAN, DARE, MAY, MOWE, MUST, NEED, SHALL, THARF, UTEN, WILL

Modals are tagged MD when there is a reading available in which a main verb is elided. In the absence of such a reading, the modal is tagged as a form of VB. 

therefore/ADV+P ye/PRO may/MD sey/VB what/WPRO ye/PRO woll/MD

therefore/ADV+P aske/VBI what/WPRO ye/PRO woll/VBP els/ADV

'/PUNC I/PRO woll/VBP well/ADV ,/PUNC '/PUNC seyde/VBD Balan/NPR
'/PUNC that/C ye/PRO so/ADV do/DOP
Modal or verb? DARE and NEED are tagged as modals or verbs depending on their syntactic context. When they take infinitival TO complements or occur with DO support, they are tagged as verbs. Otherwise, they are tagged as modals. 
I dared/VBD to say it .			I dared/MD not say it .
I do not dare/VB (to) say it .		I dare/MD not say it .

They need/VBP to eat now .		They needed/MD not come .
They do not need/VB to come .		They need/MD not come .

Modals as main verbs. Untensed modal verbs, which are attested into Modern English (generally CAN, MOWE), are tagged MD0.

xalt/MD mown/MD0 askyn/VB what/WPRO +tu/PRO wylt/MD

Participles

Adjectival vs. verbal participles. Adjectival uses of present and past participles (INTERESTED, INTERESTING) are distinguished from verbal uses and tagged as ADJ.

In doubtful cases, participles are tagged as VAG or VAN.
