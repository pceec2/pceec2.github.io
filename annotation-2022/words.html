AS WELL AS

When AS WELL AS corresponds to the correlative conjunction BOTH ... AND, it is annotated as similarly as possible to a conjunction structure. The entire conjunction is enclosed in appropriate phrasal brackets with a dash tag as appropriate to the context. The AS WELL phrase attaches like BOTH would. The phrase corresponding to the first conjunct is enclosed in phrasal brackets of its own. When the second AS phrase is not adjacent to WELL, it attaches the way a CONJP would and is associated with an *ICH* trace in the AS WELL phrase. When the second AS phrase is adjacent to WELL (as becomes more frequent over time), it attaches as a daughter of the AS WELL phrase in line with general principles concerning comparative constructions.
